Monhinga

Description

Mohinga is a traditional Burmese dish that is often considered the national dish of Myanmar (formerly known as Burma). It is a fish soup or stew made with catfish, lemongrass, ginger, onions, garlic, and other spices, served over thin rice noodles. Mohinga is typically eaten as a breakfast food in Myanmar, but it can also be found throughout the day at street vendors and restaurants. It is a popular and flavorful dish that is deeply rooted in Burmese cuisine and culture.

Ingredients

  • Catfish
  • Rice noodles
  • Lemongrass
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Oil
  • Cumin powder
  • Coriander powder
  • Paprika
  • Turmeric powder
  • Fish sauce
  • Chickpea flour (or gram flour)
  • Water
  • Lime wedges
  • Fried onion flakes
  • Cilantro leaves
  • Boiled egg (optional)

Steps

  • Begin by cleaning and cutting the catfish into large pieces.
  • Boil the catfish in a pot of water until it is cooked through, then remove the fish from the water and set it aside.
  • Using the same pot of water, add sliced lemongrass, sliced ginger, and crushed garlic to make the soup base. Bring the water to a boil and let it simmer for about 30 minutes.
  • In a separate pan, heat oil and saut√© chopped onions until they are translucent.
  • Add cumin powder, coriander powder, paprika, and turmeric powder to the onions and cook for a minute.
  • Add the onion and spice mixture to the soup base and stir well.
  • Mix chickpea flour with water to create a slurry, then add it to the soup base and stir well to thicken the soup.
  • Add fish sauce to the soup and mix well.
  • Finally, add the cooked catfish pieces to the soup and let it simmer for a few minutes.
  • To serve, place cooked rice noodles in a bowl and ladle the soup over the noodles. Garnish with lime wedges, fried onion flakes, cilantro leaves, and a boiled egg if desired.
