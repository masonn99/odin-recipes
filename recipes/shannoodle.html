Shan Noodle

Description

Shan Noodle is a traditional dish from the Shan State of Myanmar (formerly known as Burma). It is a simple yet flavorful noodle dish made with rice noodles, chicken or pork broth, and topped with a variety of ingredients such as sliced meat, bean sprouts, coriander, garlic oil, and crispy fried pork rinds. The dish is often served with a side of pickled mustard greens and a bowl of clear broth on the side. Shan Noodle is a popular street food in Myanmar and can be found in many local markets and street stalls throughout the country. It is known for its unique combination of flavors and textures, and is a must-try for any food lover visiting Myanmar.

ingredients

  • rice noodle
  • pork or chicken
  • tomato sauce
  • chickpea flour
  • fish sauce
  • toppings such as fried garlic, chopped scallions, cilantro, lime wedges

Steps

  • Soak the rice noodles in cold water for 30 minutes, or until they are soft. Drain and set aside.
  • Heat the oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat.
  • Add the onion and garlic and cook until they are fragrant and soft.
  • Add the chicken or pork to the pan and cook until browned on all sides.
  • Add the chopped tomatoes, soy sauce, fish sauce, and chili flakes to the pan. Stir well and cook until the tomatoes are soft and the sauce is fragrant.
  • In a small bowl, mix the chickpea flour with 1/4 cup of water until it forms a smooth paste.
  • Add the chickpea flour paste to the pan and stir well. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.
  • Add the rice noodles to the pan and toss to coat them in the sauce. Cook for 1-2 minutes, or until the noodles are heated through.
  • Season the Shan noodle with salt and pepper to taste.
  • Serve the Shan noodle hot, garnished with chopped scallions, cilantro, crispy fried garlic, and lime wedges.
